Space Agencies

  • ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation)

    Founded in 1969, ISRO is India’s national space agency known for pioneering cost-effective space missions like Chandrayaan and Mangalyaan. It has expanded its capabilities towards manned missions like Gaganyaan and international collaborations to advance space technology.

  • NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration)

    Established in 1958, NASA leads the United States in space exploration with landmark programs like Apollo and Mars rover missions. Currently, NASA is active with the Artemis Moon program, Mars exploration, Earth sciences, and many international partnerships.

  • ESA (European Space Agency)

    Formed in 1975, ESA is a coalition of 22 European member states collaborating on a range of missions including the Rosetta comet mission, Mars Express, and Earth observation. ESA champions international cooperation in space science and technology.

  • JAXA (Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency)

    Established in 2003, JAXA is Japan’s national space agency known for missions including the Hayabusa asteroid sample returns and contributions to the International Space Station. JAXA also plans lunar and Martian exploration with cutting-edge technology.
