Venus

Venus is the second planet from the sun and is often referred to as Earth's twin due to their similar size and mass. However, that's where the similarities end. Venus has a thick atmosphere composed mainly of carbon dioxide, which traps heat and makes it the hottest planet in the solar system.

Venus has no moons.

Moons of Venus

Venus has no natural satellites, or moons.

Rovers Sent to Venus

The Soviet Union's Venera program sent several rovers to Venus, including Venera 7, which became the first spacecraft to land on another planet in 1970.
